## Dress Categories

### Qipao (旗袍)
A body-hugging Chinese dress originating from 1920s Shanghai. Key features include:
- Mandarin collar (3-5 cm height)
- Form-fitting silhouette (requires 20+ body measurements for custom tailoring)
- Side slits for elegant movement
- Frog buttons (knotted closures, 2-3 hours of handwork per button)
- Luxurious fabrics: silk, brocade, satin
- Symbolic patterns with hand embroidery (200-500 hours per garment for premium pieces)

The global Qipao market is valued at approximately $2.8 billion.

**Qipao Styles:**
- Traditional Floor-Length — formal occasions, weddings
- Modern Cocktail — knee-length, contemporary events
- Wedding Qipao — heavily embroidered, red/gold ($5,000-$50,000)
- Evening Gala — sophisticated, luxurious fabrics

### Cheongsam (长衫)
Same garment as Qipao — "Cheongsam" is the Cantonese pronunciation, while "Qipao" is Mandarin. Hong Kong tailoring tradition involves 36 body measurements with 2mm tolerance and 3-4 fittings.

Made famous by Maggie Cheung in "In the Mood for Love" (2000), where she wore 23 different Cheongsam designs.

**Pricing Range:**
- Ready-to-wear: $100-$300
- Made-to-measure: $300-$1,500
- Fully custom/bridal: $2,000-$80,000+

### Hanfu (汉服)
"Clothing of the Han people" with over 4,000 years of history. Key characteristics:
- Cross-collar design (Y-shaped, right over left — a marker of Chinese civilization)
- Wide flowing sleeves (30cm to over 1 meter)
- Sash belt (no buttons or zippers traditionally)
- Layered garments (2-5 layers)
- Natural fabrics (silk, cotton, linen)
- Symbolic colors tied to the Five Elements theory

The modern Hanfu movement has 8-10 million active participants, with the market valued at 10 billion yuan (~$1.4 billion USD).

**Cultural Appropriation Note:** Hanfu is for anyone appreciating its beauty, regardless of ethnicity. The global community includes enthusiasts from 50+ countries.

## Dynasty Styles

### Tang Dynasty (618-907 CE)
The golden age of Chinese fashion. Chang'an was the world's largest city with 1 million+ residents.

**Characteristics:** High-waisted designs, wide flowing sleeves, vibrant colors, sheer silk layers, Silk Road influences

**5 Main Styles:**
1. **Ruqun** — High-waisted top and skirt ensemble (most iconic)
2. **Round Collar Robe** — Unisex, practical for daily wear
3. **Banbi** — Half-arm jacket, layered over Ruqun
4. **Daxiushan** — Wide-sleeve ceremonial robe
5. **Hufu** — Foreign-style riding dress (Silk Road influence)

### Song Dynasty (960-1279 CE)
Known for Neo-Confucian restraint, proportion, and intellectual depth. GDP was 25-30% of global wealth.

**Characteristics:** Soft celadon greens, pale blues, ivory whites, muted earth tones, clean lines

**5 Main Styles:**
1. **Beizi** — Open-front outer coat (versatile, layered)
2. **Moxiong Ruqun** — Chest-wrapping top with skirt
3. **Song Ku** — Wide-leg trousers (gender-neutral)
4. **Daopao** — Scholar's robe
5. **Bijia** — Sleeveless vest

Song Dynasty styles are considered the "most modern-friendly traditional style" due to their clean lines and neutral colors.

### Ming Dynasty (1368-1644 CE)
Population reached 160 million at peak. Strict color hierarchy codified by law.

**5 Main Styles:**
1. **Mamian Qun** — Horse-face skirt (now the single most trending traditional Chinese garment globally)
2. **Aoqun** — Cross-collar top with pleated skirt
3. **Pipa Sleeve Jacket** — Narrow sleeves with distinctive cuff shape
4. **Bijia** — Sleeveless vest (formal/casual)
5. **Feiyu Fu** — Flying fish robe (imperial court)

### Han Dynasty (206 BCE-220 CE)
The dynasty so influential that ethnic Chinese are called "Han people" and traditional dress is called "Hanfu." Population ~60 million, rivaling the Roman Empire.

**5 Main Styles:**
1. **Shenyi** — Deep robe (one-piece, most formal)
2. **Quju** — Curved-hem wrapping robe
3. **Zhiju** — Straight-hem robe (practical daily wear)
4. **Ruqun** — Jacket and skirt combination
5. **Paofu** — Scholar's long robe

## Occasions

### Wedding
Traditional Chinese weddings feature red as the primary color, symbolizing luck and prosperity.

**5 Bridal Styles:**
1. **Xiuhe Suit** — Two-piece with dragon-phoenix embroidery
2. **Longfenggua** — Dragon-phoenix gown (Cantonese tradition)
3. **Bridal Red Qipao** — Modern elegance
4. **Ming Dynasty Bridal Hanfu** — Historical grandeur
5. **Tang Dynasty Bridal Hanfu** — Imperial splendor

**Accessories:** Phoenix Crown, Gold Hairpin, Round Fan, Red Bouquet, Jade Pendant, Gold Earrings

**Ceremony Guide:** Tea Ceremony, Wedding Banquet Entrance, Pre-Wedding Photography

### Tea Ceremony
Recommended colors: Pale green, cream white, soft blue, earth tones
Recommended styles: Song Dynasty Beizi, Simple Qipao, Modern Hanfu
Tips: Natural fabrics, minimal accessories, subdued colors, avoid strong perfumes

### Chinese New Year
Red is the most auspicious color. Avoid black, white, and green.

**Recommended Styles:**
1. Classic Red Qipao
2. Modern Hanfu
3. Tang Dynasty Ruqun

Men traditionally wear Tang suit (唐装). Non-Chinese wearing traditional dress is generally welcomed as cultural appreciation.

### Graduation
Colors: Navy blue, red, white, champagne
Republic-era student wear (民国学生装) is the most historically appropriate style.
Tips: Incorporate school colors, maintain formality, schedule separate photo sessions.

### Engagement Photos
Most popular: Xiuhe Suit with gold dragon-phoenix embroidery
Couples typically do 2-3 outfit changes
Colors: Red, gold, champagne, blue

### Prom
Chinese-inspired dresses blending tradition with contemporary style
Features: Mandarin collar, side slits, rich colors, embroidery/beading
Popular colors: Red, black, navy, emerald green, blush pink
